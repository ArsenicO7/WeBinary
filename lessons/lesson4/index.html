Lesson 4: Multiplying and Dividing in Binary

Just like for addition, binary can be operated on with multiplication and division like decimal. As stated in the previous lesson, mathematical operations are most easily done by using binary to decimal conversion. For example, let’s say we want to multiply the binary numbers 101 and 10 together. We can do this by converting them to decimal first and then multiplying the converted numbers for the final answer in decimal. 101 from binary to decimal is 4 + 1 = 5 and 10 from binary to decimal is 2. 5*2 = 10, so 101 * 10 in binary is 10 in decimal. The binary value for 10 is 1010, so 101 * 10 = 1010 in terms of only binary.
